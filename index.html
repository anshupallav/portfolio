About Me

I’m Anshu Pallav, an Enterprise GIS Solutions Architect with over a decade of experience designing, deploying, and optimizing geospatial systems for complex business environments. I specialize in building scalable GIS infrastructures that integrate cloud technologies, automated data workflows, and cutting-edge visualization tools to support critical operations in energy, utilities, and government sectors.

Currently, I lead enterprise GIS initiatives at Harvest Midstream through LOGIC Solutions Group, where I architect high-availability systems, automate data pipelines, and deliver actionable insights through platforms like ArcGIS Pro, Survey123, Power BI, and Microsoft Azure. My work bridges strategic IT planning and real-world field operations—whether it's modernizing infrastructure with DevOps, integrating drone and mobile data collection for field workflows, or developing executive dashboards that drive data-informed decisions.

My core expertise lies in the Esri ArcGIS suite, where I have orchestrated end-to-end implementations—from geospatial data management and GIS architecture to custom application development. I am also well-versed in integrating cloud technologies to enhance performance, scalability, and cost-efficiency for enterprise-level GIS deployments.

With past roles at Esri and Avineon, I’ve delivered enterprise-grade GIS solutions across the U.S. and internationally, backed by a strong foundation in software development, data modeling, and spatial analytics. I'm a certified GISP, AWS Cloud Practitioner, and hold multiple advanced Esri certifications. My passion lies in creating intelligent geospatial ecosystems that are robust, user-centric, and future-ready.

The Post Graduate Diploma in Geoinformatics (PG - DGi) aims to provide conceptual knowledge on GIS, remote sensing and related fields, and hands-on training in GIS, remote sensing data interpretation, digital image processing, digital photogrammetry, digital cartography and GPS. Geoinformatics/Geomatics is the synergy of multiple disciplines, namely, GIS, remote sensing, photogrammetry, cartography, GPS and geodesy. Geomatics deals with spatial and non-spatial data, their methods of acquisition, management, analysis, display, and dissemination.
